Revitalizing Tradition: A Modern Redesign for the Barbary Coast Cloggers

Background

The Barbary Coast Cloggers, a San Francisco-based Appalachian clogging and folk dance team, approached us to overhaul their outdated website. The primary objectives were to correct outdated information, integrate new photos and videos, and give the site a contemporary look that aligns with the dynamic spirit of the dance team.

Issues/Problems

One significant challenge was the lack of clear direction at the project's outset. This necessitated a lot of independent research and creative freedom. Additionally, sourcing up-to-date media required reaching out to various team members and combing through different platforms like YouTube.

Process/Action/Decisions

Given the minimal constraints, I began by researching other dance websites for inspiration. I developed a simple wireframe to organize content placement and then proceeded with the design using HTML and CSS. The process involved: Creating a visual layout that reflected the group's vibrant energy. Integrating new media, including photos and embedded YouTube videos. Developing essential pages such as About, Class Information, Media, and Contact Us.

Work

The redesigned website was well-received by the Barbary Coast Cloggers. They appreciated the modernized color scheme and updated fonts, which better reflected their identity. However, the initial design did not include mobile responsiveness, which is a planned future improvement.

Key Features

Multi-page Design:

Added comprehensive pages including Home, Class Information, Upcoming Events, Gallery with photos and video links, Cue Sheets, and Contact Us.

Media Integration:

Embedded YouTube videos and updated photos to showcase the team's activities.

Contact Form:

Implemented a functional email inquiry form for easy communication.

Result

The client was pleased with the overall redesign, noting the improved aesthetic and functionality. Moving forward, the website will require updates to accommodate new content as the group continues to perform and evolve. Additionally, making the site responsive for mobile users is a priority for the next phase of the project.
